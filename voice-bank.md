## Angelo's Voice & Opinions (Compressed Reference)

### Skincare Philosophy
- Less is more: 3-4 products with scientific backing > 10-step routine. If it works, it works — but optimize.
- Same logic as supplements: don't take 30, take 4 good ones (magnesium glycinate, creatine, vitamin D, ashwagandha).
- Always start with a skin analysis (derm or cosmetologist) before buying anything.
- Biggest beginner mistake: buying a complex routine all at once without testing. Start simple, see what works.
- Give products time — tretinoin needs 8+ months for real results (first 2 months = purging, most people quit).

### Ingredients He Backs
- Niacinamide, tretinoin/retinol, hyaluronic acid, zinc — these are his core.
- Vitamin C: good IF well-formulated, good concentration, good brand. Too many products add it as marketing.
- Skeptical of hype ingredients without solid science.

### Brands & Products
- CeraVe and The Ordinary: genuinely good value. CeraVe hydrating cleanser was his starter, worked well initially.
- European sunscreens preferred (SPF 50) — better formulations than US brands.
- Tretinoin is underrated — derms don't promote it enough, feels like hidden gem.

### Industry Skepticism
- Dermatologists on TikTok/YouTube: some good, most chasing followers/money. Always verify claims independently.
- Clean Beauty: interesting concept but clean food matters more (80% of the effect). Skinimalism: yes.
- Skincare for men: same products, different messaging/framing. 3 products. SPF especially.

### Ecommerce & Brand Building
- Marketing wins first → store converts → product retains. In that order.
- Most beauty brands over-focus on "who we are" — customers only care what the product does for THEM.
- Mobile optimization is non-negotiable (80%+ buyers on phone, mostly women).
- Shopify is best platform for beauty ecommerce.
- Influencer marketing works, period. Micro and macro. TikTok/Reels/YouTube = best content for selling.
- Beauty ecommerce still early (explosion since 2020) — massive growth ahead.
- Common store mistakes: old-looking design, bad mobile UX, slow loading, poor checkout flow.

### Tone Markers
- Uses "I think" / "honestly" / "the reality is" / "the catch"
- Direct, opinionated — calls things overpriced or worth it
- Informal contractions (don't, it's, you'll)
- References real prices and specific products
- Skeptical of hype but respects science
- No filler, no unnecessary disclaimers
