Dry skin isn't just about moisturizer. Most people with dry skin apply a heavy cream and wonder why their skin still feels tight an hour later. The issue is usually that you're skipping the layers that actually hold moisture in place.

## The Dry Skin Problem

Dry skin has two related issues: it produces less sebum (oil) than other skin types, and its barrier often has lower ceramide levels, meaning it loses water faster. The technical term is transepidermal water loss (TEWL). Applying a heavy cream addresses one part of this equation but not both.

The solution is layered hydration — building from water-attracting ingredients (humectants) up to water-sealing ingredients (occlusives), with ceramide-rich moisturizers in between.

## Morning Routine for Dry Skin

**Step 1 — Cleanser**: Skip the foam. Foaming cleansers strip the skin's natural oils, which dry skin cannot afford. Use a cream or oil cleanser, or even just rinse with lukewarm water in the morning if your skin isn't dirty.

Best options: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser (~$15), La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser (~$16).

**Step 2 — Hydrating Toner or Essence**: Apply while skin is still slightly damp. This prep step saturates the skin with water that your next products will help seal in.

Best options: Klairs Supple Preparation Unscented Toner (~$22), COSRX Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence (~$25).

**Step 3 — Moisturizer with Ceramides**: This is the core of a dry skin routine. You need something rich enough to compensate for the lack of natural oils but not so occlusive it feels suffocating during the day.

Best options: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream (~$18) for budget, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Double Repair Face Moisturizer (~$22) for something lighter, Tatcha The Water Cream (~$70) if you want a premium option with an elegant texture.

**Step 4 — SPF**: Essential, and for dry skin, choose an SPF with moisturizing ingredients rather than a separate moisturizer + lightweight SPF. ISDIN Eryfotona Actinica SPF 100 (~$60) is incredible for dry skin. More affordable: EltaMD UV Physical SPF 41 (~$36).

## Evening Routine for Dry Skin

**Step 1 — Cleanser**: Same as morning, or use a gentle oil cleanser to remove SPF and any product buildup without stripping.

**Step 2 — Serum**: For dry skin, the most valuable serum is hyaluronic acid — specifically, a multi-weight HA formula that hydrates at different skin depths. Apply to slightly damp skin.

Best options: The Inkey List Hyaluronic Acid Serum (~$10), SkinCeuticals H.A. Intensifier (~$105) if you want the premium version with proven results.

**Step 3 — Retinol (2-3 nights/week)**: Dry skin can use retinol but benefits from a buffer approach — apply moisturizer first, then retinol on top, then moisturizer again. This reduces penetration slightly and minimizes the dryness that retinol can exacerbate.

Start with a low concentration: Differin 0.1% (~$14), or CeraVe Resurfacing Retinol Serum (~$22).

**Step 4 — Rich Moisturizer**: Go heavier at night. Your skin repairs itself while you sleep, and there's no makeup or sunscreen to apply over it.

Best options: Cetaphil Rich Night Cream (~$18), First Aid Beauty Ultra Repair Cream (~$36), or a dedicated balm like Weleda Skin Food (~$20) for very dry skin.

**Step 5 — Occlusive to seal (optional but powerful)**: A thin layer of Vaseline or Aquaphor over your moisturizer creates a seal that dramatically reduces overnight water loss. Sounds heavy, but it absorbs into a matte finish by morning. This one step transformed many dry skin routines.

## Ingredients to Look For

- **Ceramides** (1, 3, 6-II) — rebuild barrier lipids
- **Hyaluronic acid** — attracts and holds water in skin
- **Glycerin** — humectant, extremely effective
- **Squalane** — lightweight oil that mimics skin's natural sebum
- **Shea butter, cocoa butter** — rich occlusive emollients for very dry skin
- **Urea** (5-10%) — helps skin hold water AND has mild exfoliating properties at higher concentrations

## What to Avoid

Alcohol in toners and serums — it's drying and damages barrier function.

Fragrances — especially synthetic fragrance, common cause of irritation in dry, sensitized skin.

Harsh exfoliants too frequently — dry skin can exfoliate 1x per week at most. More than that and you're removing the surface cells your skin is relying on for protection.

## The Simple Version

If this feels overwhelming: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, a drop of The Inkey List HA serum on damp skin, CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, and SPF. That's $50 total and will work better than most elaborate routines.

Add retinol and occlusive sealing at night when ready. Build from there.
