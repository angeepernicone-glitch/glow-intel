Men's [skincare](/blog/skincare-routine-acne-prone-skin) has been overcomplicated by marketing. "For men!" labels on the same product in different packaging, sold at a different price point. Let me skip all of that.

[Skin](/blog/how-to-layer-skincare-actives-correctly) is skin. The same ingredients that work for women work for men. The difference is in messaging, routine simplicity, and a few actual skin characteristics that do matter.

## What's Different About Male Skin

Men's skin does have biological differences worth acknowledging:
- Thicker skin (about 25% thicker on average) — takes longer to show age but also takes longer to respond to actives
- More sebaceous gland activity — oilier overall
- Shaving — a form of physical exfoliation but also a regular source of barrier stress and irritation
- Less consistent routine behavior — the simpler the routine, the higher the adherence

The product lineup doesn't change much, but the texture preferences often do — lighter, gel-based formulations tend to work better than heavy creams.

## The Three-Product Core

If you'll only do three things: a decent cleanser, moisturizer with SPF, and one active. That's it.

**Cleanser**: CeraVe Hydrating Facial Cleanser (~$15) is the answer for most skin types. [Oily skin](/blog/skincare-routine-oily-skin) does better with a gel cleanser — CeraVe Foaming Facial Cleanser (~$15) or La Roche-Posay Toleriane Purifying Foaming Cleanser (~$16). Wash twice a day, morning and before bed.

**Moisturizer with SPF**: This is the single product recommendation I make more than any other. A moisturizer with built-in SPF means one less step and eliminates the most common male skincare omission — no SPF. CeraVe AM Facial Moisturizing Lotion SPF 30 (~$18) is the straightforward answer. Kiehl's Ultra Facial Cream SPF 30 (~$38) if you want something that feels more premium.

**Active (niacinamide)**: One serum. Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1% from The Ordinary (~$7). Apply before moisturizer. Reduces oil, minimizes pore appearance, fades any post-breakout marks. It's the highest return-per-dollar product in skincare.

**Total cost: ~$40. Total time: 4 minutes per day.**

## The Five-Product Routine (For Those Who Want More)

For men who want to push further — especially for anti-aging or acne:

**Morning**: Cleanser → Niacinamide serum → SPF moisturizer (or moisturizer + separate SPF 50).

**Evening**: Cleanser → Retinol (2-3x per week) → Moisturizer.

That's it. The retinol addition is the single biggest upgrade for long-term skin improvement. Start low — 0.3% or 0.5% — use it 2-3 nights per week, and expect some mild purging in weeks 2-4. Push through it.

## Post-Shave Specific Advice

Shaving exfoliates and irritates the skin, especially dry-shaving or electric shaving. Post-shave, apply:
- A simple alcohol-free toner or calming mist (Heritage Store Rosewater ~$10) to hydrate immediately
- Your niacinamide serum — it's anti-inflammatory and helps with razor irritation and ingrown reduction
- Moisturizer

Skip actives (retinol, AHAs) immediately after shaving. You've just removed the top layer of skin cells — adding an acid or retinol to freshly shaved skin is asking for unnecessary irritation.

## The SPF Conversation

If I could get every man to do one thing differently: wear SPF every day. Not for aesthetic reasons — for medical ones. Melanoma rates in men over 50 are significantly higher than in women. And SPF 30-50 every morning reduces that risk meaningfully.

European sunscreen formulations remain superior to US options due to EU's expanded approved filter list. La Roche-Posay Anthelios Ultra-Light Mineral SPF 50 is available in the US and uses non-whitening filters that work for most skin tones.

If you hate sunscreen texture: EltaMD UV Clear SPF 46 (~$40) is invisible on skin, doesn't leave a white cast, and is one of the lightest formulas available.

## The Honest Message

Men's skin doesn't need a 10-step routine or "specially formulated for men" products. It needs consistent basics: cleanser, active, moisturizer, SPF.

The brands marketing "Power Activ Men's Anti-Aging System" are selling you the same ingredients in different packaging at double the price. The Ordinary niacinamide, CeraVe moisturizer, and a decent SPF will do more.

Three products, twice a day, consistently. That's the whole game.
